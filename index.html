How it works

Three steps. That's the whole app.

1

Catch the impulse

About to buy something you don't really need? That's the moment Cartless is for — before the tap, not after the regret.

2

Walk away and log it

Tap "I didn't buy", jot the item and price. Not sure yet? Pause it 24–48 hours — the wanting almost always fades, and then it's a real choice.

3

Watch it stack up

The money you didn't spend grows in plain sight, and each skip moves you toward 100. Spending less becomes a win you can see.

Honest questions

Wondering if it'll actually work for you?

Is this just another app that shames me?

No. Cartless has no red numbers and no nagging. Bought something anyway? Fine. The screen celebrates what you saved, never what you spent.

I have zero willpower. Won't I just ignore it?

It doesn't run on willpower. Walking away once does the work — log it, and if you're unsure, a 24–48h pause lets the urge fade on its own. One tap, not a personality change.

Will I have to track every cent?

Never. No bank login, no budgets, no spreadsheets. You log a skip when it happens. That's the entire commitment.
